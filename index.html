Section 01
Dashboard
⭐ Today's Top Priority
Every session, the platform auto-selects the single highest-value grant you haven't engaged — factoring urgency, match score, and pipeline status. One click opens full How-to-Apply instructions.
⚡ Urgent Deadline Banner
If any grant has a deadline within 14 days, a pulsing red alert appears automatically. It names the grant, shows days remaining, and links directly to the application page.
📈 Animated Stat Cards
Five counters animate on every load: Total Grants, Act Now (urgent), Saved, Tracked, and Potential Funding from your saved grants.
🔵 Pipeline Health Ring
Circular progress indicator shows % of 40 grants engaged. Red = under 25%, Gold = 25-50%, Green = 50%+. Updates automatically as you work your pipeline.
🔍 Grant Cards
Each card shows name, region/type/deadline badges, match score bar, description, the gold "Why It Fits IAR" blurb, and a deadline countdown. Click anywhere to open the full How-to-Apply modal.
🎛️ Filters
Filter by Region, Type, Deadline status, Focus Area, and Match Score. All filters stack live. Use the search box to find any grant by name or description keyword.
Pro Tip: Start every session by reading Today's Top Priority and the Urgent Banner. These two elements alone will keep IAR's grant operation on track.

Application Tracker
📌 Pipeline Columns
Prospecting — Saved, not yet applied. Applied — Submitted, awaiting response. In Progress — Active funder conversation. Awarded — Grant won (triggers confetti!). Declined — Not funded this cycle; note feedback and reapply next cycle.

Important: Tracker data lives in your browser's localStorage. Export CSV regularly to preserve your pipeline. Do not clear browser data without exporting first.

Section 03
AI Grant Writer
📄 8 Narrative Types
Executive Summary • Statement of Need • Program Description • Expected Impact & Outcomes • Budget Narrative • Organizational Capacity • Veteran Angle / Founder Story • Letter of Inquiry (LOI). Each is 350-500 words, tailored to the specific funder.
⚙️ How It Works
Select a grant, choose narrative type, optionally paste RFP language into Additional Context, click Generate. Takes 10-20 seconds. Edit the output directly, then hit Refine with AI to polish your edits. Copy and paste into your application.
Power move: Paste specific funder language from their RFP into Additional Context before generating — the AI will hit their exact keywords and priorities.
Shortcut: Inside any How-to-Apply modal, click "Draft Narrative" to auto-select that grant in the Writer and switch tabs.

Grant Strategy for IAR
🏆 The 3-Tier Funding Strategy
Tier 1 — Quick Wins (apply now): Gupta Family Foundation, Peter G. Dodge TAPs, ACF Community Grants, Smithers Foundation. Rolling deadlines, strong mission match, apply this month to build your track record.

Tier 2 — Mid-Tier (3-6 months): Meadows Foundation, Hogg Foundation, RWJF. Relationship-based. Start LOIs now, cultivate over months.

Tier 3 — Transformational (6-18 months): Second Chance Act, MacArthur, DRK Foundation. Require track record + partnerships. Build toward these.
📖 Shawn's Story Is the Weapon
Combat veteran. Heroin addiction survivor. 100-mile ultra runner. Author. Founder. No grant writer can manufacture what Shawn has lived. Every application that leads with specific, concrete story moments outperforms one that leads with program statistics. Funders remember stories. They forget bullet points.
📊 Build Outcomes Data Now
The fastest way to improve every application: start tracking measurable outcomes today. People served per month, 30/60/90-day sobriety retention, employment placements, housing. Even 6 months of data transforms "we believe" into "we've proven."
🔄 Rejection Is a Cycle, Not a No
Most major grants require 2-3 application cycles before funding. Always request feedback when declined, incorporate it, and reapply. Log every rejection note in the Tracker. The organizations that keep applying are the ones that eventually get funded.
Velocity matters: Aim for at least one LOI, application, or funder contact per week. Grant funding rewards consistent applicants — the organizations applying regularly are the ones getting funded regularly.

Section 10
Frequently Asked Questions
How do I add a grant the AI found to my tracker? ▾
Run an AI Grant Search and click the "+ Add to Tracker" button next to each result's name. If the grant is already in the database it saves to Prospecting. If it's new it creates an entry and adds it — move it through the pipeline like any other grant.
My tracker data disappeared — how do I get it back? ▾
Tracker data is saved in your browser's localStorage. If you cleared your cache, switched browsers, or used private/incognito mode, the data won't appear. Export CSV after every session where you make changes to preserve your work.
How accurate are the match scores? ▾
Match scores are editorial estimates — how closely each funder's priorities align with IAR's mission and Shawn's background. Not algorithmically computed. Use them as directional guidance: 90%+ is exceptional, 70-80% is strong, below 70% is worth monitoring but not a priority application.
The AI Writer doesn't sound like us. What do I do? ▾
Edit the draft directly in the text area — it's fully editable. Then click "Refine with AI" and your edited version becomes the input. Also add specific IAR language, program stats, or RFP keywords into Additional Context before generating to dramatically personalize the output.
What does Open/Rolling mean? ▾
Open/Rolling means the funder accepts applications year-round with no specific deadline — typically reviewed quarterly. Great for IAR because you can apply any time. Earlier submissions in a quarter get reviewed sooner since board meetings are typically March, June, September, December.
Can I use this on mobile? ▾
Yes — the platform is responsive and works on phones and tablets. Drag-and-drop Kanban works better on desktop. On mobile, tap a tracker card to cycle its status forward instead of dragging.
How do I use the Priority Advisor? ▾
Click the gold "What Should I Do Today?" button in the bottom-right corner. A panel slides open and Claude analyzes your saved grants, tracked applications, and urgent deadlines to produce a personalized 5-bullet action plan. The advice refreshes as your pipeline changes.

Section 11
Grant Glossary
LOI
Letter of Inquiry — a 1-3 page pre-application letter many foundations require before inviting a full proposal. Your first date before a full relationship.
NOFO
Notice of Funding Opportunity — the federal government's official announcement that a grant program is accepting applications. Published at grants.gov.
RFP
Request for Proposals — similar to NOFO, used by foundations and government agencies to formally invite grant applications for a specific program.
Logic Model
A visual framework showing inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes → impact. Most federal grants require one. A roadmap from resources to results.
501(c)(3)
The IRS designation for tax-exempt nonprofit organizations. Required for almost all foundation and government grants. IAR has this status confirmed.
SAM.gov
System for Award Management — federal registration required before applying to any federal grant. Free but takes 1-2 weeks. Do this first.
Match / Cost Share
Some grants require you to match a percentage of the award with your own funds. A 1:1 match means if they give $50K, you show $50K from elsewhere.
Program Officer
The foundation staff member who manages a specific grant program. They review applications and are your most important relationship at any foundation.
Site Visit
A funder's in-person visit to your organization before making a grant decision. Treat it as the most important meeting of the funding cycle.
Sub-grant
Funding passed from a primary grantee (e.g., a state agency) to a smaller org. IAR can access federal COSSUP funds through Texas HHSC sub-grants without applying directly.
SUD
Substance Use Disorder — the clinical term for addiction. Using "SUD" in federal applications signals sophistication and increases credibility with health-focused reviewers.
TDCJ
Texas Department of Criminal Justice — the state agency managing Texas prisons. Partnership with TDCJ significantly strengthens reentry grant applications.
